In the Galician language, my final B. Sc. degree in Mathematics dissertation is called An Introduction to Modal Regression. It was directed by Rosa M. Crujeiras and Maria Alonso. It obtained the maximum qualification of 10.00/10.00 and it was awarded with a prize for its language quality.
In my dissertation, I have done a bibliographic revision of multimodal regression using the mean-shift algorithm. In the first chapter, I illustrate why mean regression is not always a good option. Then, I introduce a technical chapter where non-parametric kernel regression is explained. Finally, I developed the idea of multimodal regression and I show a real data application.
  • Unha Introdución á Regresión Modal

This small project, in French, was done in the course Data Analysis of M1 Applied Math at ENSIIE. It aimed to reproduce the clustering algorithm of k-medoids and its results in R. As shown in the article A simple and fast algorithm for K-medoids clustering by Hae-Sang Park and Chi-Hyuck Jun, this algorithm is an alternative clustering technique very useful if we know the distance matrix.
