In galician language, my final B. Sc. degree in Mathematics dissertation called An introduction to Modal Regression. It was directed by Rosa M. Crujeiras and Maria Alonso. It obtained the maximum qualification 10.00/10.00 and it was awarded with a prize for its language quality.
In my dissertation, I have done a bibliographic revision of multimodal regression using the mean-shift algorithm. In the first chapter, I illustrate why mean regression is not always a good option. Then, I introduce a technichal chapter where non-parametric kernel regression is explained. Finally, I develop the idea of multimodal regression and I show a real data application.

This small projet, in french, was done in the course Data Analysis of M1 Applied Math at ENSIIE. Its aim was to reproduce the clustering algorithm of k-medoids and its results in R. As it is shown in the article A simple and fast algorithm for K-medoids clustering by Hae-Sang Park and Chi-Hyuck Jun, this algorithm is an alternative clustering technique very useful if we know the distance matrix.
